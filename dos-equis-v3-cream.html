V3: WARM CREAM

Chad Kaleky • FTS Newsletter

In 2006, Dos Equis did something beer brands never do.

They made you want to watch the commercial.

"I don't always drink beer... but when I do, I prefer Dos Equis."

The Most Interesting Man in the World.

It tripled their sales.
It became a meme before memes were a thing.
It made a Mexican import feel legendary.

Then, in 2016, they killed it.

Retired the character. Tried new campaigns.
"Keep It Interesante."
Safe. Forgettable. Boring.

Sales flattened.

Fast forward to 2026:

He's back.

Why now? They tested it:

→ 97% brand recall — after 10 years

→ 84% of consumers wanted him back

→ The character never left — he lived on in memes

The CMO admitted it:

"Our advertising got a little bit safe. A little bit boring."

So they stopped chasing new. They went back to iconic.

The lesson:

You don't always need a new idea.
Sometimes your best asset is sitting in the archive.
If it still resonates — modernize it, don't abandon it.

Stay thirsty.

Dos Equis brought back their most iconic line.
